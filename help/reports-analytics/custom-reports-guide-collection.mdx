## What are Custom Reports?

Custom Reports is the analytics workspace in Alvys where you build dashboards from pre-built visualizations, filter and drill into your data, design your own charts with drag-and-drop, and (optionally) author advanced custom metrics. This collection brings the core guides together so you can follow them in the recommended order below, or go straight to the topic you need. Whether you call it Custom Reports, Custom Reporting, Reports V2, or Alvys Intelligence reporting, this is the starting point.

## Where to find Custom Reports?

Custom Reports lives under the **Reports** menu in the left sidebar. When Custom Reports is enabled for your company, a **Custom Reports** category (marked with a **Beta** badge) appears inside that menu and opens your dashboards. If you do not see it, Custom Reports may not be enabled for your team yet, or you may not have been granted Reporting Access; see "Set up Custom Reports" below.

## Key concepts

**Dashboards and visualizations:** A dashboard is a saved collection of visualizations (charts, tables, and tiles). You build dashboards from pre-built visualizations in the Analytics Catalog; no formulas are required to get started.

**Analytics Catalog:** The catalog of ready-made metrics, attributes, and visualizations you drag onto a dashboard or into the Report Designer.

**Reporting Access (None, Viewer, Analytic):** The per-user access level that controls what someone can do. **None** has no access; **"Viewer"** can view dashboards; **"Analytic"** can analyze, author, and export.

**Tier (Free and Custom):** On the **Free** tier dashboards are read-only and admins can share Alvys-curated dashboards. On the **Custom** tier, analytics users can author dashboards, visualizations, and scheduled exports.

**MAQL:** The metric query language used to author advanced custom metrics for the rare cases where the catalog does not already include the metric you need.

## Settings and Permissions

Enabling Custom Reports and assigning access is admin work. Admin, Partner Admin, and Support roles already have reporting access built in. Other users need a Reporting Access level assigned to them: **None**, **"Viewer"**, or **"Analytic"**. The Driver role cannot be granted Reporting Access. To enable Custom Reports for your company and assign access, follow [Set up Custom Reports](/help/reports-analytics/how-to-set-up-custom-reports).

## Limits and Behavior

On the **Free** tier, dashboards are read-only: admins can share Alvys-curated dashboards, but no one on the company can author dashboards or visualizations. Authoring dashboards, visualizations, and scheduled exports requires the **Custom** tier. Cross-filtering and drilling are available when enabled for a dashboard. Writing custom metrics in MAQL is an advanced, optional step intended for the rare cases where the Analytics Catalog does not already include the metric you need; most users never need it.

## FAQs

**Q: Where do I find Custom Reports in Alvys?**

**A:** Open the **Reports** menu in the left sidebar. When Custom Reports is enabled for your company, a **Custom Reports** category with a **Beta** badge appears and opens your dashboards.

**Q: I don't see Custom Reports in the sidebar. Why?**

**A:** Custom Reports may not be enabled for your company yet, or you may not have been granted Reporting Access. An admin can enable it and assign access by following [Set up Custom Reports](/help/reports-analytics/how-to-set-up-custom-reports).

**Q: What is the difference between Viewer and Analytic access?**

**A:** **"Viewer"** can view dashboards. **"Analytic"** can analyze, author, and export. **None** has no access to Custom Reports.

**Q: Do I need to write formulas to build a dashboard?**

**A:** No. You can build your first dashboard entirely from pre-built visualizations in the Analytics Catalog. Writing custom metrics in MAQL is an advanced, optional step for metrics the catalog does not already include.

**Q: In what order should I read these guides?**

**A:** Follow the numbered order in "**How to use Custom Reports**" section of this article, starting with "**Get started with Custom Reports,**" or jump straight to the topic you need.
